<objective>
Replace the noisy bounding-box erosion in `BorderMaskCleaner` with a
topologically-meaningful Region of Interest (ROI) anchored on the
SingularityDetector's Core. Drop spurious minutiae produced by the
Gabor ringing artefact using the same source of truth the rest of
the pipeline already populates.

Purpose: eliminate the 91+ false-positive terminations outside the
print's central area that the current bounding-box erosion cannot
catch, without coupling the post-processors to any specific detector
class.
Output: a fully declarative pipeline where the ROI flows through the
context and any new post-processor that wants the ROI can read it
without `isinstance` checks.
</objective>

Debug evidence (recorded during 08-03):
* Real fingerprint (100__M_Left_index_finger): 22 candidates inside
  the SingularityDetector ROI (13T + 9B) vs 101 outside (91T + 10B).
* The 91 "outside" terminations are Gabor ringing artefacts at the
  bottom edge of the print; they are topologically real
  ``CN=1`` pixels on the skeleton but topologically meaningless.
* Current ``BorderMaskCleaner`` uses morphological erosion of the
  quality-mask bounding box; it cannot drop these because the
  ringing pixels still sit inside the bounding box.
</context>
